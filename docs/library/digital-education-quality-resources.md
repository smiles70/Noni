## 1. Quality Matters (QM) Higher Education Rubric — Seventh Edition

**Type:** Industry-standard, peer-reviewed design rubric  
**Publisher:** Quality Matters (QM) Program, MarylandOnline, Inc.  
**Edition:** 7th Edition (2023)  
**URL:** https://www.qualitymatters.org/qa-resources/rubric-standards/higher-ed-rubric

**What it is**  
A comprehensive set of 42 specific standards across 8 categories used to evaluate and guide the design of online and blended courses. The rubric is informed by educational research literature regarding factors that improve student learning and retention.

**Key categories**
- Course Overview & Introduction
- Learning Objectives (Competencies)
- Assessment & Measurement
- Instructional Materials
- Learning Activities & Learner Interaction
- Course Technology
- Learner Support
- Accessibility & Usability

**Why it matters for Noni**  
The QM rubric is the closest thing to a standardized "quality bar" for digital curriculum. Its emphasis on clear learning objectives, accessibility, and learner support directly maps to geragogy principles (cognitive protection, progressive disclosure, human authority).

**How to cite**  
> Quality Matters. (2023). *Higher Education Rubric Standards (7th ed.)*. MarylandOnline, Inc. https://www.qualitymatters.org/

## 2. OECD Digital Education Outlook 2023

**Type:** International governmental policy publication  
**Publisher:** Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD)  
**Year:** 2023  
**URL:** https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/oecd-digital-education-outlook-2023_c74f03de-en.html

**What it is**  
A comparative, thematic analysis of how OECD countries shape their digital education ecosystems. Covers governance frameworks, digital competencies for teachers, assessment platforms, and data governance in education.

**Key themes**
- Digital education governance (procurement rules, regulatory frameworks)
- Teacher digital competencies
- Learning management systems and assessment platforms
- Equity and inclusion in digital education
- Data governance and privacy

**Why it matters for Noni**  
Provides the macro-level policy context for quality standards. The OECD's framework for assessing "maturity" of digital transformation in education teaching (Chapter 3) offers a vocabulary for quality benchmarking that is internationally recognized.

**How to cite**  
> OECD. (2023). *Digital Education Outlook 2023: Towards an Effective Digital Education Ecosystem*. OECD Publishing. https://doi.org/10.1787/232c9631-en

## 3. UNESCO — Six Pillars for the Digital Transformation of Education

**Type:** Global policy framework  
**Publisher:** United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO)  
**Year:** 2024 (endorsed by UNESCO Global Education Coalition)  
**URL:** https://unesdoc.unesco.org/ark:/48223/pf0000391299

**What it is**  
A common framework built on six pillars to guide the digital transformation of education worldwide. Developed collaboratively with the Digital Transformation Collaborative and the Global Education Coalition.

**The six pillars**
1. Inclusive and equitable digital transformation
2. Connectivity and infrastructure
3. Digital competencies and content
4. Data and technology governance
5. Innovation and emerging technologies
6. Partnerships and collaboration

**Why it matters for Noni**  
Pillar 3 (Digital competencies and content) provides the closest thing to a global standard for what quality digital curriculum should achieve. The framework's emphasis on inclusion and equity aligns with geragogy's learner-centered approach.

**How to cite**  
> UNESCO. (2024). *Six Pillars for the Digital Transformation of Education*. UNESCO Digital Transformation Collaborative. https://unesdoc.unesco.org/ark:/48223/pf0000391299

## 4. Gates & Wilson-Menzfeld — Geragogy in Digital Skills Programs (Systematic Review)

**Type:** Systematic narrative review (peer-reviewed)  
**Authors:** Jessica R. Gates & Gemma Wilson-Menzfeld  
**Journal:** *International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health* (IJERPH)  
**Year:** 2022  
**URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9364233/

**What it is**  
A systematic narrative review of 17 international papers (2010–2020) examining how digital skills programs for middle and older adults are implemented, and whether geragogy/critical geragogy theory is present in their delivery.

**Key findings**
- Geragogy is rarely *explicit* in program design but is often implicit through empowerment-focused delivery.
- Three dominant themes emerged: negative perceptions of aging, the learning environment, and value of technology.
- Digital skills programs that succeed with older adults create safe learning environments and emphasize the *personal value* of technology over abstract utility.

**Why it matters for Noni**  
This is the most rigorous academic synthesis specifically on digital skills education for older adults. The finding that "personal value of technology" drives engagement directly supports Noni's principle-based curriculum design.

**How to cite**  
> Gates, J. R., & Wilson-Menzfeld, G. (2022). What role does geragogy play in the delivery of digital skills programs for middle and older age adults? A systematic narrative review. *International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health*, *19*(15), 9147. https://doi.org/10.3390/ijerph19159147

## 5. Formosa & Fragoso — Bridging Geragogy and Pedagogy (2025)

**Type:** Peer-reviewed academic article  
**Authors:** Marvin Formosa & Ana Fragoso  
**Journal:** *Educational Gerontology* (Taylor & Francis)  
**Year:** 2025  
**URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/03601277.2025.2569386

**What it is**  
A research synthesis arguing for a "learning-sciences-based approach" to older adult education that bridges geragogy and pedagogy. Draws on a case study from Concordia University Montreal's CAE (Continuing Adult Education) programme.

**Key arguments**
- The field of older-adult learning has grown significantly, but debates over *quality of instruction* remain underdeveloped.
- Integrative geragogy (combining pedagogy, andragogy, and geragogy) produces better outcomes for older learners than any single theory.
- Quality assessment for older adult education must account for cognitive changes, life experience as prior knowledge, and the emotional dimension of technology learning.

**Why it matters for Noni**  
This is the most recent peer-reviewed synthesis explicitly calling for *quality standards* in older adult digital education. The argument that quality assessment must account for "emotional dimension of technology learning" is a direct design input for Noni's confidence-preserving tone and geragogy contract.

**How to cite**  
> Formosa, M., & Fragoso, A. (2025). Bridging geragogy and pedagogy: Towards a learning-sciences-based approach to older adults' education. *Educational Gerontology*. https://doi.org/10.1080/03601277.2025.2569386
